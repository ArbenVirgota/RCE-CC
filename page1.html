STRUCTURE

Prof. Ir. Bambang Hari Kusumo, M.Agr.St., PhD

Respected figure, leader, or influencer who supports and advocates for the goals and mission of the Centre For Environmental Research and Climate Change..

Dr. Ir. Sukartono, M.Agr
(Director)

It involves overseeing and managing the operations, policies, and strategies of the Centre For Environmental Research and Climate Change. 

Some common responsibilities may include:
Setting the organization's vision, mission, and long-term objectives.
Formulating and implementing policies to achieve organizational goals.
Managing and supervising department heads or other high-level officials.
Ensuring compliance with regulations and legal requirements.
Representing the organization in official functions and interactions with other entities.
Allocating resources and budgetary planning.
Making critical decisions that impact the organization's overall performance and direction.

Prof. Dr. Ir. I Gusti Putu Muliarta Aryana, MP
(Institution in charge)

Responsible for overseeing all activities related to environmental research and climate change studies conducted by the institution. This could include responsibilities such as:

Setting the center's research agenda and priorities in environmental and climate change areas.
Leading and coordinating research projects and initiatives related to environmental issues and climate change impacts.
Managing the center's personnel, including researchers, scientists, and support staff.
Securing funding and resources to support the center's research activities.
Engaging with government agencies, non-governmental organizations, and other stakeholders to collaborate on research and address environmental challenges.
Representing the center in conferences, meetings, and public events related to environmental research and climate change.

Ir. L. Arifin Aria Bakti, M.Agr
(Chairman)

The specific duties and responsibilities of a RCE&CC Chairman : 

Leadership: Providing overall leadership and guidance to the organization's staff and volunteers.

Strategic Planning: Developing and implementing strategic plans that align with the organization's mission and vision.

Resource Management: Overseeing the allocation of financial and human resources to support the organization's programs and projects.

Stakeholder Engagement: Building and maintaining relationships with key stakeholders, including government agencies, NGOs, universities, and community groups.

Fundraising: Identifying and securing funding sources to sustain the organization's operations and initiatives.

Advocacy: Advocating for environmental issues and policies that align with the organization's mission.

Program Development: Working with the team to develop and manage environmental education programs, research projects, and community engagement initiatives.

Reporting and Accountability: Ensuring that the organization meets its goals and objectives, and providing regular reports to the board of directors, funders, and other stakeholders.

Public Relations: Representing the organization in public forums, conferences, and media interactions.

Siska Ita Selvia, ST., M.URP
(Secretary)

Some common duties associated with this role:

Environmental Policy Development: Developing and implementing environmental policies, strategies, and regulations to address environmental issues such as pollution control, natural resource conservation, climate change mitigation, and biodiversity protection.

Regulatory Compliance: Ensuring that organizations and individuals comply with environmental laws and regulations. This may involve monitoring and enforcing environmental standards and permits.

Environmental Assessment: Conducting environmental impact assessments (EIAs) for proposed development projects to evaluate their potential environmental effects and recommend mitigation measures.

Resource Management: Managing and preserving natural resources, including land, water, air, and biodiversity. This can involve conservation efforts, sustainable resource management, and protection of sensitive ecosystems.

Climate Change Mitigation: Developing and implementing strategies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, adapt to climate change impacts, and promote renewable energy and energy efficiency.

Environmental Education and Outreach: Raising public awareness about environmental issues and promoting environmental education and sustainability initiatives.

Interagency Coordination: Collaborating with other government agencies, departments, and stakeholders to ensure a coordinated approach to environmental protection and sustainability.

Research and Analysis: Conducting or supporting environmental research, data collection, and analysis to inform policy decisions and assess environmental trends.

International Agreements: Participating in international environmental agreements and negotiations to address global environmental challenges.

Budgeting and Resource Allocation: Managing the budget and resources allocated to environmental programs and initiatives.

Environmental Emergency Response: Coordinating responses to environmental emergencies, such as natural disasters or industrial accidents that pose environmental risks.

Report and Communication: Preparing reports, briefing documents, and communications to inform the public, policymakers, and stakeholders about environmental issues and progress.

Dr. Misbahhuddin, ST., MT.
(Training and Education)

These efforts are essential for addressing pressing environmental challenges and promoting a more environmentally responsible society. Here are key aspects of environmental training and education:

Formal Education:

School Curriculum: Environmental topics are integrated into school curricula at various levels, from primary to tertiary education. Subjects like environmental science, ecology, and sustainability are commonly taught.
Degree Programs: Provides support to specialized degree programs in environmental science, environmental studies, and related fields.
Research Opportunities: Higher education institutions often provide opportunities for research and innovation in environmental science and related disciplines.

Informal Education:

Environmental Workshops and Seminars: These events provide hands-on learning experiences and opportunities for discussion and networking.
Museum Exhibits and Nature Centers: Institutions often host exhibits and programs that educate the public about the environment and conservation.
Online Resources: The internet offers a wealth of information and resources for self-guided learning about environmental topics

Professional Training:

Environmental Training Programs: These are designed for professionals and organizations to enhance their knowledge of environmental regulations, sustainability practices, and best environmental management practices.
Certifications: Professionals can earn certifications such as LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) for sustainable building practices.

Community and Outreach Programs:

Community Workshops: Local organizations and government agencies often organize workshops on topics like recycling, composting, and energy conservation.
Environmental Clubs and Organizations: Clubs and volunteer groups promote environmental awareness and organize community cleanups and conservation projects.

Environmental Awareness Campaigns:

Public Awareness Campaigns: Governments, NGOs, and corporations often run campaigns to inform the public about environmental issues and promote sustainable behaviors.
Social Media and Media Outreach: Information about environmental issues is widely disseminated through digital and traditional media channels.

Environmental Ethics and Values:

Education emphasizes the importance of ethical considerations in environmental decision-making, including concepts like environmental justice and intergenerational equity.

Hands-On Learning and Field Trips:

Field trips to natural habitats, parks, and conservation areas provide experiential learning opportunities for students of all ages.

Environmental Policy and Advocacy Training:

Training programs help individuals and organizations understand environmental policy, advocacy strategies, and how to engage with policymakers to effect change.

Continuing Education and Lifelong Learning:

Environmental education is not limited to formal schooling; it extends to lifelong learning opportunities for individuals of all ages and backgrounds.
Global Initiatives: International organizations, such as the United Nations, promote global environmental education initiatives to address global challenges like climate change and biodiversity loss.

Dr. Andi Chairil Ichsan, S.Hut
(Consulting)

Environmental Assessment and Planning: Environmental consultants assess the environmental impact of proposed projects or activities. They conduct environmental impact assessments (EIAs) and provide recommendations for minimizing or mitigating adverse effects on the environment. Consultants also assist in the development of environmental management plans.

Regulatory Compliance: Consultants help clients navigate complex environmental regulations and ensure compliance with local, national, and international environmental laws. They assist in obtaining permits and licenses necessary for operations.

Site Investigation and Remediation: Environmental consultants investigate contaminated sites and design remediation strategies to clean up pollution. This includes assessing soil and water quality, conducting risk assessments, and developing cleanup plans.

Sustainability and Green Practices: Consultants advise organizations on adopting sustainable practices and implementing environmentally friendly initiatives. They may help with energy efficiency improvements, waste reduction, and the adoption of renewable energy sources.

Hazardous Materials Management: Environmental consultants specialize in the handling, storage, and disposal of hazardous materials, including hazardous waste management plans and compliance with hazardous materials regulations.

Water Resources Management: Consultants work on water-related projects, such as watershed management, water quality assessments, and the development of water conservation strategies.

Air Quality Monitoring: Consultants assess air quality, monitor emissions, and help organizations reduce air pollution through emission control strategies.

Environmental Due Diligence: In mergers, acquisitions, or property transactions, environmental consultants conduct environmental due diligence assessments to identify potential environmental liabilities and risks.

Risk Assessment: Consultants evaluate environmental risks associated with various projects and advise clients on risk management strategies.
