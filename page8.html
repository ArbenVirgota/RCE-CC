FAQS

What is Research Center of Environment and Climate Change?

A Research Center of Environment and Climate Change, often referred to as an Environmental and Climate Change Research Center, is an institution or department within University of Mataram that focuses on conducting research, studies, and projects related to environmental issues and climate change. These centers typically bring together researchers, scientists, and experts from various disciplines to address and explore a wide range of topics related to the environment and climate change.

What are specifics goals of RCE&CC?

The specific goals and objectives of such research centers that include:

Environmental Conservation: Studying ways to protect and conserve natural ecosystems, wildlife, and biodiversity.

Climate Change Mitigation: Researching strategies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and combat climate change.

Adaptation Strategies: Investigating how communities and ecosystems can adapt to the impacts of climate change, such as rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and sea-level rise.

Sustainability: Promoting sustainable practices in various sectors, including agriculture, energy, transportation, and urban planning.

Policy and Advocacy: Informing policymakers and the public about environmental and climate issues to drive evidence-based decision-making and advocacy efforts.

Education and Outreach: Providing educational programs and resources to raise awareness about environmental and climate issues.
